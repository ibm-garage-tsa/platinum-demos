PREPARE TO GIVE THE DEMO

The setup process is illustrated in the following chart. Each number icon corresponds to the respective preparation step below.

1 - Provision your demo environment

The Automation Decision Services (ADS) demo is simply based on the Telco retention sample provided in the default ADS install.
You must install CP4BA 21.0.2 or provision ROKS environment that will be available for 2 weeks at no charge.
This document explains how to get a free pre-configured Cloud Pak for Business Automation environment:

Reserve your CP4BA preconfigured environment on ROKS
  1. Log in to the IBM Technology Zone using your IBM ID. Then, click on the Activation Kits tab.



  1. Scroll down to click on the Cloud Pak for Business Automation tile.



  1. Click on the Environments - Reserve Here! tab. Then, select the Reserve Here! - Cloud Pak for Business Automation OpenShift Demo Environments tile.

  2. Click on the Preinstalled OpenShift Environments tab. Then, select the latest version of the Preinstalled CP4BA on Red Hat OpenShift on IBM Cloud.

  3. Select Reserve now. Then, after entering the Purpose, Preferred Geography, and Notes, click on Submit.

  4. When you are done, you will see a confirmation message. In addition, you will receive a confirmation email with all the access links.





  1. Then, you will receive an email confirming that your instance is provisioned. It may take 30 to 45 minutes.

Access your provisioned CP4BA environment

  1. Log in to the IBM Technology Zone.

  2. At the top right corner, click on the Profile icon. Then, click on Reservations. You will then be directed to the My reservations page, in which you can see your reservation details. Note: Bookmark this page for ease of reference.





  1. Once its provisioned, your instance should look like the screenshot below.



Go to top

Access your OpenShift instance

When your instance is provisioned, you should receive a confirmation email with the access links. If the links are not attached to the email, please refer to the steps below.

  1. Go to the START HERE section in your OpenShift Instance. Note: The link is also provided in the confirmation email.



  1. Click to open the README.md file, and scroll down to click on Preinstalled Cluster. Under Instructions, click on 2 - Wait for your cluster to expand the section and follow step 2.2.

  2. From your Reservation page, double-click on your instance, and copy your Environment ID.



  1. Log in to your IBM Cloud account.

  2. On the top menu, swap to the DTESQUAD account.



  1. From the Clusters menu, search for your cluster using the Environment ID that was copied from step 3.



  1. Check that your clusters state is green, and double-click on it. Check that your Ingress subdomain is set up, and open your OpenShift web console.

  2. Open the Workloads menu, and select Config Maps. Then, set your project to dtecp4ba.

  3. Search for access to access the icp4Adeploy-cp4ba-access-info file. Open this file to access all your access links and credentials to the various environments. Note: You have the ADS-runtime-access-info in that file as well.



  1. The important link ie the BAStudio URL. From this URL, you will be able to access the key elements of the demo:
    Business Automation Studio
    Application Designer
    Automation Decision Services

  2. You are now ready to set up the demo. Open BAStudio from the ADS link in the access file.

2 - Watson Studio setup

Set up your Watson Studio account

  1. Click here to log in to your IBM Cloud account, or create a new one.

  2. Click on Catalog at the top, and type Watson Studio in the search menu. Then, select Watson Studio Lite.





  1. Select a location for your service. Select the Lite plan (free), and click Create.

  2. Once its provisioned, click on Get Started.

Go to top

Create your deployment space
  1. Click on New deployment space.



  1. Give a name to your deployment space (e.g., [your name]-deployment-space).

  2. Create your Cloud Object Storage service.
    a. Go in the Select object service section, and click Create.
    b. Select the Lite version, and click Create.

  3. Select your machine learning (ML) service.
    a. Go in the Select machine learning service (optional) section, and click Create. Then, select your location.
    b. Select the Lite version, and click Create.
    c. Click on Select a machine learning service, and scroll to select your newly created ML service.
    d. Click on Create (bottom right of the screen), and let the deployment space creation run.
    e. Once its done, you should see a The space is ready message. Click on View new space to see your new deployment space.





Go to top

Check the services installed
  1. Click on the Menu icon (top left corner).



  1. Click on Services, and then select Service instances.



  1. You should now see your 3 newly created services.



Go to top

Create a new project

  1. Click on IBM Watson Studio, and then click on New project.

  2. Select **Create an empty project, and enter details for your project. Once its created, youll have an empty project.

  3. Click on Add to project and add the AutoAI experiment.





  1. Enter a name for your experiment. Example: LOT-Customer retention demo experiment

  2. Click on Associate a Machine Learning service instance.

  3. Select your ML service instance, and click on Associate service in the blue ribbon.



  1. In the AutoAI configuration menu, click on Reload. Your ML service should now show up.

  2. At the bottom right corner, click on *Create to create your ML experiment in Watson Studio. Once its completed, your AutoAI experiment is ready to be set up.

Go to top

Set up your churn ML service
  1. Under Add data sources, click on Browse.



  1. Select the customer-churn-data.csv file. Note: This data file was already downloaded as one of the prerequisites.



  1. Select the prediction criteria by following the steps below.

a. Click on Select prediction column.



b. Select CHURN.



c. Click on Run experiment. Your AutoAI run will begin, and it should take around five minutes. Once its done, the progress maps status will read Experiment completed.







d. Click on the first row in the Pipeline leaderboard, and then select Save as.



e. Select Model, and click on Create. Then, you should see a Saved model successfully notification.



f. Return to the project view by clicking on the ML experiment in the breadcrumb navigation bar. You should now see your Watson Machine Learning (WML) model listed.





Go to top

Set up the LTV ML service
  1. Follow the steps below to add the Customer LTV CSV to your data assets.

a. Click on New Data asset, and select browse.



b. Select the customer-LTV-data.csv file. Note: This data file was already downloaded as one of the prerequisites.



c. The file should now appear in your list under Data assets.



  1. Follow the steps below to create a new AutoAI experiment.

a. Click on New AutoAI experiment, and enter details for your experiment. Make sure the ML service is associated to your experiment. Then, click Create.



b. Under Add data sources, click on Select from project. Select the customer-LTV-data.csv file, and click on Select Asset.





c. Under the What do you want to predict? section, select LTV.



d. Click on Run experiment. The experiment should last around five minutes. Once its done, the progress maps status will read Experiment completed.





e. Click on the first row in the Pipeline leaderboard. It is marked with a black star on the left column. Then, click Save as.



f. Select Model, and click on Create. Then, you should see a Saved model successfully notification.



g. Click on the project to return to the project view. You should now see that the model has been created and listed.





Go to top

Deploy the models in the deployment environment

Your free quota will be consumed as long as the models are deployed.
Deploy the models before the demonstration, and undeploy them as soon as the demo is done to not be overcharged and to save your free monthly quota.

Deploy the LTV service
  1. Select the LTV model and click on the ellipsis icon. Then, click on Promote.



  1. Select your deployment space that was previously created. Click on Promote.

  2. Repeat steps 1 and 2 with the customer churn model.

  3. Click on IBM Watson Studio (top left corner). Select Deployments and then View all spaces.

  4. Select the Spaces tab, and click on your deployment space.

  5. In the Assets tab, you should see your two models that are ready to be executed.

  6. At this point, nothing is deployed yet. You will be charged as soon as you execute the models. If you click on the Deployments tab, there should be no deployments to monitor.

  7. In the Assets tab, select the LTV model and click on the rocket icon to start the service.

  8. Give a name to the deployment, and click Create. When its deployed, you should see the deployment message.

  9. Repeat steps 6 to 10 for the customer churn model.

  10. Click on the Deployments tab to see the services that are deployed. You will be charged until you undeploy the services after the demonstration.

  11. Get the Deployment ID to configure ADS. In the Deployments list, click on Churn Service. Then, copy the Deployment ID. Repeat step 12 to get the Deployment ID for the LTV service as well.
