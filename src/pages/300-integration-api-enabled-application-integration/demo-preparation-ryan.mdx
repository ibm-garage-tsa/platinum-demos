### **DEMO INSTALLATION AND SETUP**

<summary>1 - Provision a Cloud Pak for Integration environment</summary>
<br/>

Install Cloud Pak for Integration, or provision a ROKS environment. To reserve your ROKS instance, follow these steps: <br/><br/>

1. To reserve a preinstalled Cloud Pak for Integration (CP4I) ROKS cluster on Red Hat OpenShift, go <a href="https://techzone.ibm.com/my/reservations/create/62f1112aeeb0ab0017dc7608" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">here</a> and complete the form. <br/>

3. You will receive a second email once the environment is provisioned. You should expect this to take between 30 and 120 minutes. This email contains information, such as URLs, allowing you to connect to the instance.<br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image006.png" width="800" />If you have issues connecting to your instance, please contact <a href="https://ibm-cloud.slack.com/archives/C0124J683GW" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">#itz-techzone-support</a>.

After you are logged in, click <a href="https://cloud.ibm.com/catalog/content/ibm-cp-integration-72f63273-f2f6-4e9c-8626-60fe798c57be-global" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">here</a> to install Cloud Pak for Integration.<br/><br/>Make sure you are in an **ITZ** account (1) and the **Product version** is **2022.4.1** (2).<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/Prep2.2.png" width="800" /><br/>

3. Scroll down to the **Select your cluster** section. From the list, select the **Redhat Openshift Cluster** (1) that you just created. Then click **Add project** (2).<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image2.3.png" width="800" />

5. Scroll down to the **Set the deployment values** section and toggle the **Use an existing secret?** slider to **no** (1). Enter a value for **csDefaultAdminPassword** (2). 7. <summary>Set up Salesforce</summary>

<br/>

Let’s set up Salesforce. You will need a developer account to use for testing. If you already have a Salesforce developer account, you can use that (start at step 5 below). If not, you can sign up for a free developer account by following steps 1 - 4 below.

<br/>

1. Go to <a href="https://developer.salesforce.com" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Salesforce Developers</a>.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-dev-signup.png" width="800" /><br/>

2. Click **Login** (1), and then select **Salesforce** (2).<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/Prep3.1.png" width="800" /><br/>

3. Click **Try for free** or **START MY FREE TRIAL**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/Prep3.2.png" width="800" /><br/>

4. Follow the prompts on the following Saleforce pages to get your free developer account.<br/>

5. As soon as you have your account, go back to the <a href="https://login.salesforce.com/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer">Salesforce log in page</a>. and log in to your dev admin account.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/Prep3.5.png" width="800" /><br/>

6. To get your Salesforce integration URL, click your **user profile**. The URL text below your account name is your login URL. Copy and paste it somewhere for later use in this demo.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-url.png" width="800" /><br/>

7. Next, you need to retrieve a security token. For this, click your **user profile** and select **Settings** in the profile panel.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-settings.png" width="800" /><br/>

8. Under **Settings**, find and click **Reset Security Token**. Then, click **Reset Security Token**, and the newly-generated security token will be emailed to you.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-reset-token.png" width="800" /><br/>

9. Next, you will create an Application representing App Connect Enterprise, and retrieve the Consumer Key and Consumer Secret. Click the **setup cogwheel** at the top right.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-setup.png" width="800" /><br/>

10. In the navigator on the left-hand side, scroll to **PLATFORM TOOLS**, expand **Apps** (1), and click **App Manager** (2).<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image3.7.png" width="800" /><br/>

11. Click **New Connected App**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image3.8.png" width="800" /><br/>

12. Enter **App Connect** (1) as the **Connect App Name** and your email as the **Contact Email** (2). Select **Enable OAuth Settings** (3).<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image3.9.png" width="800" /><br/>

13. Select **Enable for Device Flow** (1). Now select **Manage user data via APIs (api)** (2) as the **Selected OAuth Scopes**. Click **Add** (3)<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image3.10.png" width="800" /><br/>

14. Click **Save**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/prep-image3.11.png" width="800" /><br/>

15. It will take approximately ten minutes for the new connected app to register in Salesforce. Once it does, you should see **Manage Consumer Details** displayed. Click **Manage Consumer Details,** following any authentication directions.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-manage-consumer.png" width="800" /><br/>

16. Copy and save the **Consumer Key** and **Consumer Secret.**<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/salesforce-key-secret.png" width="800" /><br/>


Your Salesforce developer account is ready.

1. Click on the **Home** icon, and **Manage catalogs**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/home-manage-catalogs.png" width="800" /><br/>

2. Open **Catalog for Demo use**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/catalog-for-demo-use.png" width="800" /><br/>

3. Open **Catalog settings**, then **Portal**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/catalog-settings-portal.png" width="800" /><br/>

4. Here you can see your **Portal URL**. Open a new browser tab, and paste your Portal URL.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/portal-url.png" width="800" /><br/>

5. On the Portal page, click **Create account**.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/create-account.png" width="800" /><br/>

6. Complete the form. Suggestion: use devuser as username, and you are welcome to use your personal email address.<br/><br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/complete-form.png" width="800" /><br/>

7. To complete your registration, you need to open your email and accept the registration.

  <InlineNotification>If you are using mailtrap, you need to open your mailtrop inbox and accept the registration.</InlineNotification>

  <br/><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ibm-garage-tsa/platinum-demos/master/src/pages/300-integration-api-enabled-application-integration/images/mailtrap-inbox.png" width="800" /><br/>

Congratulations, your portal developer user is created and ready for the demo.
